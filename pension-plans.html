UNDERSTANDING THE BASICS

Why You Need Pension Plan

1. Government Pension And PPF Are Not Adequate

Statistics show that more than 91% of retired people are financially dependent on their children. And the rest of the retired people have hardly met their day's needs by the government pension or aid from their previous company. Older people are more prone to get sick and may need medical care which could be quite expensive. So keeping these points in mind it is important to plan one’s retirement in a proper way by investing in a good retirement plan.

2. Increase in Human Life Expectancy

According to WHO the average human life expectancy is 68.8 years. With the improving medical facilities, the average life expectancy of the people has increased and so is the medical expense. Medical expenses are expected to double due to the rise in inflation. So the best way to cope with inflation and manage the health-related expenses post-employment is through a retirement plan.

3. Being Self-Dependent

As people are moving towards nuclear families, they are on their own and are financially independent. So the best way to be self-dependent after retirement is by investing in a retirement plan. Having a retirement plan will provide financial assistance during retirement.

4. Achieve your Dream and Goals

Are you striving hard to achieve your dreams? Most of us strive to fulfil our children's dreams and spend a whole lot of money for them, but this is done at the cost of sacrificing our life goals. Consider saving for yourself to live comfortably and also fulfil your child's dream at ease by investing in insurance plans.

5. Guaranteed Income

The pension plans provide generate guaranteed income. As per the IRDAI rule, the insurance companies should provide a non-zero return on all premiums. Thus the insurance companies are bound to offer a guaranteed income of 1% at the least of the total premium paid.

Types Of Pension Plan

The pension plan is classified into various categories to cater for the needs of the insurance seekers. These plans are classified based on the plan structure and benefits. The following are the types of pension plans

  1. Deferred Annuity
  2. Immediate Annuity
  3. Annuity Certain
  4. With Cover Pension Plan & Without Cover Pension Plan
  5. Life Annuity
  6. National Pension Scheme
  7. Pension Funds
  8. Guaranteed Period Annuity Plan
  9. Whole Life ULIPs
  10. Defined Benefit
  11. Defined Contribution
Plan Type Features
Deferred Annuity It refers to a single premium payment or systematic premium payment and the money is accumulated. After completion of the policy tenure, the pension is provided. It offers tax benefits. The amount is locked and partial withdraw is not allowed.
Immediate Annuity Only lump sum investment is allowed. The pension will be provided instantly after paying the premium. The premium paid has tax benefits. The nominee receives the pension amount in case of the death of the policyholder.
With Cover Pension Plan And Without Cover Pension Plan Under With Cover Plan, they have a life cover option. The nominee receives a lump sum amount as the death benefit after the demise of the policyholder. The life cover amount is not large as most of the premium paid goes towards growing the corpus.
Under without cover plan, there is no life cover to the policyholder. In case of the policyholder’s demise, the nominee will get the corpus.
Annuity Certain Here the annuity amount is paid to the annuitant for a specific number of years. The insured can choose the number of years (eg: 65-70 years). The beneficiary will receive the amount after the demise of the insured.
Life Annuity Here the pension amount is given to the insured until death and if the option ‘with spouse’ is chosen then the pension will be received by the nominee
National Pension Saving Scheme This is a Government based scheme. It was introduced by the government to secure an individual’s financial status after retirement. As per the policyholders preference the money is invested in equity and debt funds to generate returns for the money. The insured can withdraw 60% amount on retirement and the rest 40% is used to purchase an annuity.
Pension Funds This plan offers better returns upon maturity. The Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority PFRDA has authorised 6 companies as fund managers to manage Pension Funds.
Guaranteed Period Annuity Plan The annuity is disbursed for a period of 5/10/15/20 years, whether or not the insured survives the policy period
Whole Life ULIPs Here the amount invested stays untouched for the whole life and upon retirement partial withdrawal can be made. The income obtained is tax-free. Additional withdrawn is allowed whenever required by the insured.
Defined Benefit This type of benefit is practised in an organisation where the employer also contributes to your pension amount.
Defined Contribution Here the income of retirement is not guaranteed. Here the both you and your employer can contribute to the pension plan. On retirement, the accumulated amount relies on the contribution made. This retirement cash can be utilised to produce a retirement remuneration.

Key Features of Pension Plan

Pension Plans are specifically designed with distinctive features, especially meant for people who wish to plan for retirement and do not wish to depend on anyone for financial needs post-retirement years.

Let us discuss the important features of the pension plan.

Sum Assured

The Sum assured is the most important feature of a pension plan. The sum assured is provided as the death benefit or maturity benefit. The sum assured amount can be 10 times the annual premium amount or a lump sum fund value of the policy bought. The sum assured depends on the type of pension plan taken. If there is no sum assured then it is considered as a pure pension plan instead of an insurance plan with a pension scheme.

Accidental Death Benefit Immediate and Deferred Annuity

Most of the pension plans come with an annuity attached: It can be deferred or immediate annuity. Based on the requirement of the policyholder the type of annuity can be chosen. The immediate annuity pays the pension plan immediately after receiving the lump sum premium. In the case of a deferred annuity, the insurer starts paying after a few years. The key advantage of this feature is that you can choose as per your needs.

Accumulation of Funds and Payment Period

The Accumulation period is where the insured pays regular premiums to the insurance company. You can either pay the premium in a lump- sum or in periodic intervals. The invested premium gets accumulated and provides financial security for the future. The accumulation period is the time frame between the start date of investment until the end date. This policy tenure determines the amount of money accumulated.

Surrender Value of Pension Plan

The surrender value refers to the amount payable by the insurance company to the policyholder if they decide to surrender the policy before its maturity date. In this case, the policyholder will lose all the benefits.

Vesting Age

The vesting age refers to the age at which the policyholder starts receiving their pension amount. The vesting age differs from policy to policy. It usually ranges from 50 years or 70 years or even 90 years.

Factors Affecting

Returns On Pension Plans

Having Retirement planning right from the start is very crucial! Everyone wants to lead a stress-free, comfortable and secure old age where they do not have to worry about the funds each time for their basic needs and comfort. A good retirement plan helps to fulfil one's needs at the age when one wants to retire and are not able to work any longer.

Now, the question running through your mind is how can we determine the returns of pension? What are the factors affecting the returns on pension plans?

Here are some of the important factors considered that can help you to choose the correct pension plan.

Based on Annuity

Based on the type of annuity option you choose i.e. deferred or immediate, your pension plans returns would differ. In a deferred plan, the funds get accumulated first in the accumulation period and can be withdrawn in the payment period, as a lump sum amount, quarterly or yearly basis. In the case of an immediate plan, you will start getting the returns immediately as soon as you pay the one single premium amount. The returns are based on the choice of the annuity.

Plans that are subject to Market Risk

Certain pension plans like the ULIP Pension plans are based on debt or equity mutual funds. Therefore, they are subject to market risk which directly affects your returns. The performance of the market whether up or down will affect your returns. For example, if a defined amount of your pension investment was sent to stocks, and if the price of the stocks you invested went high, then you will gain higher returns, and vice versa.

Start Investing in Pension Early

According to research, saving at least 15% or even more of your income, starting from an early age for 10-20 years in your career can ensure that you have saved enough money to retire comfortably at 60. It is not important that you save a high amount for a retirement plan; it means saving substantially by investing a small amount systematically, starting from an early age or when you start earning your income.

Investing Period

The returns depend on the number of years you remain invested in a pension plan. A longer period will give you better returns. In case you need to withdraw some money from your investments you can withdraw 1/3rd amount from the vesting corpus amount.

Tax Benefits

Most of the time the taxes can significantly reduce the vesting corpus amount you have available for you when you retire. So it is important to learn about how your pension plans are going to be taxed. Though the pension plans mention that they provide the tax benefit of U/S 80(C), you should be aware that only a portion of the money is not taxable, but the rest of the pension received is Taxable. Not every premium that is paid or any withdrawals that are made are tax exempted. Some income, like interest, is being taxed as per income tax rates.

If You Retire at 60

How Much Retirement Funds Do You Require?

Most of us in India have a predominant mindset to retire at 59 years, but in western countries, they have a concept of retiring as early as 40 years of age. They aim at working hard spending consciously and retire rich and early. So this movement type is called Financial Independence Retire Early (FIRE). Financial Independence doesn’t mean saving a lot of riches it means paying off all the debts by the time you retire and aim at getting passive income.

So let us discuss how much passive income do you require by the time you reach 59.

Let us assume that Mr Raju is an employee who is 40 years of age and has a monthly income of Rs. 80,000 per month. He is the sole earner of the family. His current monthly expenses are 50,000 including the loan and insurance premiums. Considering the above-given information the retirement amount can be easily calculated using a pension calculator that is available online.

As per RBI, the current inflation during the year 2021-2022 is 5.1%. So keeping the inflation at 5% the corpus amount can be calculated.

Current Monthly Income = Rs. 80,000

Monthly Expenses = Rs. 50,000

Current age = 40 years

Retirement age = 60 years

No: of years left for Retirement  age = 20 years

Inflation = 5%

Estimated post-retirement expenses = Rs. 1,32,665/ month

So for a monthly retirement income of Rs Rs. 1,32,665/ month, you need to invest Rs. 18,768 / month starting at the age of 40.
The amount to be invested per month would decrease if the retirement planning is started early.
