Different Types of

Two-Wheeler Insurance Policies in India

Two-wheeler insurance is offered under two categories.

1. Third Party or Liability Cover

  • Third-party insurance cover is mandatory according to Motor Vehicles Act and driving without this insurance is punishable in India.
  • This insurance policy will help you to cover the damages done to the third party’s vehicle or property that is caused due to an accident by your vehicle.
  • It also covers medical bill expenses incurred due to injuries by the third party and thereby reducing your financial liability.
  • This policy comes with cheap premium rates.

2. Comprehensive Insurance Policy

  • A comprehensive insurance policy provides financial assistance under all kinds of unforeseen events involving your two-wheeler.
  • It provides coverage for repair of damages caused to your vehicle as well as the third party’s vehicle and property. It also covers the legal expenses, medical bills expenses to treat injuries, also provides money for disability or death, also covers losses caused due to theft, vandalism and even natural disasters.
  • The policy also offers various add-on features like No Claim Bonus (NCB), Zero Depreciation Cover, Accessories cover etc. that gives wholesome insurance coverage for your vehicle.
  • When compared to a third-party insurance policy It is expensive, but it helps in providing substantial financial cover to save you from unexpected losses.
  • Even though it is not mandatory to have a comprehensive policy, it is advisable to buy it to safeguard your financial interests.

Features of Bike Insurance!

One should purchase two-wheeler insurance not because it is mandatory under the law, but because it provides a lot of benefits and features that come in handy in case of any unforeseen events like accidents, theft or any natural disaster.

Accidental Coverage

A comprehensive bike insurance policy will cover all your financial expenses incurred on repairing your vehicle caused due to an accident. Even small repairs and minor damages are covered at a fraction of a cost, thanks to the insurance.

Coverage Against Theft/vandalism

If your bike gets damaged due to theft or robbery or due to a riot, a two-wheeler insurance policy will take care of such disasters and will save you from losing your hard-earned money owing to factors out of your control.

Third-Party Liability

The comprehensive policy has the feature to have various add-on covers to provide a wholesome shield against unforeseen expenses. You will be rewarded by the insurance company in the form of (NCB) for safe driving and not making any claims throughout the year. Your next two-wheeler insurance premium comes cheaper to you.

Coverage Against Natural Disasters

If you are in a zone that is prone to natural calamities like floods, earthquakes, storms, etc, your comprehensive policy will provide coverage for any damage caused due to these calamities.

Add-On Covers For

Your Two-Wheeler Insurance!

While the standard two-wheeler insurance policy will cover all the basic expenses incurred on your two-wheeler due to an accident or other unforeseen circumstances, there might be some sudden expenses that are not covered under the basic coverage and will make a big hole in your pocket. To safeguard from such events, add-on covers are available as per your needs.

Add-on covers are also called riders benefit, which provides additional coverage on your existing two-wheeler insurance policy that you can purchase by paying an extra premium. Your insurance provider offers different add-ons wherein you can select the ones that meet your requirements.

Here are the popular add-ons offered by the insurance provider:
1

Zero Depreciation Cover

As the age of the vehicle increases, depreciation causes the value of the vehicle to go down. In case of an accident, the insurance provider will cover the repairs and damages based on the depreciation value that can be extremely less if it’s an old vehicle. This is where the zero dep add-on cover comes in handy, which ensures the vehicle is calculated as a new vehicle.

2

Road Assistance

This add-on will help in case your vehicle breaks down in the middle of the road in a secluded place, by providing towing facility, cashless garage facility, accommodation benefits for any other emergencies and the likes.

3

Accident Cover for Pillion

This add-on is important as it provides coverage for medical expenses incurred in case a pillion rider is injured in an accident. It covers hospitalisation expenses for the pillion rider in the event of partial/permanent disability and some cases even death.

4

Accessories Coverage

Sometimes you may have to change or add electrical/non-electrical accessories to your vehicle. The cost for the accessories will be borne by the insurance providers.

5

Daily Allowance Coverage

Under this allowance, your insurer will supply daily allowance cash for your travel expense until your insured vehicle is repaired at one of the network garages.

6

Return to Invoice Cover

In case your vehicle encounters total loss the insurance provider will pay the Insured Declared Value (IDV) of the vehicle. But with this add-on, you will be paid the purchase price of the vehicle including the registration and taxes without the IDV value.

7

Helmet Cover

This add-on will enable you to receive a new helmet in case your helmet was damaged due to the accident.

8

EMI Protection

Your insurance provider will pay for the EMI of your bike in case the vehicle is getting repaired in one of the network garages.

The add-ons are just optional but they are very useful and strengthen your insurance profile. You may opt for some or all of them as per your requirements.

What is not Covered?

Exclusions

There are certain circumstances where your policy may not cover the expenses. The exclusions and clauses of the insurance plan have to be read thoroughly before buying the policy.
Here are a few situations where the insurance company will not cover:

  • Any damage/ loss caused due to regular wear and tear to the vehicle
  • Any damage/ loss caused when driven by a person without a valid driver’s license
  • Any damages/loss caused due to electrical or mechanical breakdowns
  • Depreciation of the vehicle
  • Damage to tyres and tyre tubes
  • Any damages/loss caused if the person is found to be driving under the influence of liquor/alcohol/drugs
  • Any damages/loss occurred to the vehicle when the policy has expired or is not active.
  • Damage/Loss caused due to war /mutiny /nuclear risks.
In case of claim

Claim Process For Bike Insurance!

The most important feature of an insurance company is the claim settlement ratio. An individual should purchase a plan based on a high claim settlement ratio. The claim process should be hassle-free. Every insured person expects the claim process to pass through and get all the reimbursement without any rejections.

Let us discuss the process involved to settle your claim without any issues.

  • Take a note of the vehicle number involved in the accident and their registration numbers.
  • Take a quick snap of the accident spot and damaged vehicle with your camera. (These proofs are necessary)
  • Immediately inform your insurance agent or provider about the accident.
  • Now lodge a complaint and make an FIR at the nearest police station, if there is an utmost need, like physical injury to a third party, property damage to a third party, or death of a third party involved in the accident.
  • FIR complaint is required even if your bike is stolen.
  • Visit the insurer's website and fill the online claim form available.
  • The concerned representative will contact you within 24 hours to have your claim registered. They will provide a claim registration number to keep track.
  • A surveyor from the insurance company will be sent to inspect the vehicle before repair.
  • Repair at the nearest network garage.

In Case of Cashless claim settled

Following additional documents will be needed

  • Registration Certificate Copy
  • Tax Receipt Copy of your bike
  • Valid two-wheeler insurance Documents
  • Valid Driving License Copy
  • FIR copy is required in case of theft, third-party injury, death or property damage.

In Case Vehicle Repaired on Your Own

Additional documents will be needed in case you get your vehicle repaired on your own and file for a reimbursement claim

  • Bills and receipts of repaired and replacement parts

In Case of Bike Theft

Following additional documents will be needed

  • Duly verified R.T.O. Paper
  • NOC letter in case of pledged bike
  • Letter of Subrogation and Indemnity issued by a notary

In Case of Third-Party Claims

Following additional documents will be needed

  • Copy of the legal notice sent by the third party.
  • Original documents for verification.

Once the filing of the claim is done with all the needed documents, forms duly filled, and a green signal is given by the vehicle surveyor, then your insurance provider will approve your claim.
The claims made and approved will be settled through NEFT or Cheque.

What is Renewal

Renew Two-Wheeler Insurance Online Easily!

It is important to renew two-wheeler insurance as per the law and to keep yourself financially safe. If your policy has expired or is about to expire renewing your insurance is an easy process, you can get renew either at your insurer’s website or insurejoy.com.

Let us understand the renewal process better with various scenarios:

Things to do Before the Policy Expires

  • If your policy is about to expire contact your insurer and learn about the premium amount. The premium amount differs every year. Also, discuss the required documents needed for renewal and arrange for an agent to meet and collect the information from you
  • Alternatively, you can save your time by logging in to the insurer’s website to check the information
  • If you wish to select any add-ons you can prefer to add them to your portfolio while renewing the policy
  • The premium amount payment can be made through cash or cheque while opting for offline mode. But, if you are buying it online, just make instant payment through net banking and get the policy renewed immediately

Things to do After the Policy Expires

You need not worry even after the policy has expired as you still have a small time frame, called a grace period, to get it renewed. The grace period is usually from 15 to 30 days after the expiry date but, not every insurer offers this period. Some insurers may offer renewal for free during this period and but, some may incur levy charges. The following steps have to be followed if your policy lies within grace period:

  • Check if there is any late fee incurred and if any, make that payment
  • Try to clear all past dues and pending premiums
  • Choose the policy you want to buy and select any add-ons you want to add to your renewed policy
  • Furnish the details and submit the required documents to the insurer. You can make the payment either through an online transaction or through an agent and the renewed policy will be delivered to you

Renewal Outside Grace Period

If you fail to renew the policy within the grace period you will lose your two-wheeler insurance policy. You might not be eligible for renewal with the same insurer. You will lose all the previous benefits and may have to buy a new insurance policy altogether.

Drawbacks of Not Renewing Policy On Time:

  • Will be charged with higher premiums
  • Complete loss of previous benefits
  • Heavy fines levied
  • Fresh Inspection of The Vehicle from the insurance company
  • No NCB (No Claim Bonus)
  • Driving without insurance is punishable by the law and will lead to a lot of legal trouble with police authorities
  • Huge expenses will be incurred in case you meet an accident without an insurance policy

Details Required for Two-Wheeler Insurance Policy Renewal:

  • Your Name (As on official documents)
  • Your Contact Number and E-mail id
  • Vehicle Registration Number And RC copy
  • The Existing Insurance Policy Number

Factors That Affect

Your Bike Insurance Premium

Certain factors affect the premium of your two-wheeler insurance policy which is discussed below:

  • Bike Model

    A high-end expensive bike will attract a higher premium compared to an economical bike. The premiums are based on three categories for bikes when it comes to their cc: 0-150cc, 150-350cc and above 350cc. The premium amount rises as the cc goes up.

  • Manufacture Year of the Bike

    The cost of repair and damages for the new bikes is higher as the parts is going to be expensive. So the newer bike has a high premium. New bikes have higher Insured Declared Value (IDV), so at the time of reimbursement, a higher amount has to be settled in case of accident or theft. However, older bikes have lesser IDV and thereby attract lower premiums.

  • No Claim Bonus

    If you do not file for any claims in a single year you are eligible for a No Claim Bonus (NCB) in the following year. The NCB discounts start from 10% and go up to 50% on the premium. You can also transfer your NCB from one policy to another!

  • Your Township

    If you live in an urban and densely populated area you will attract a higher premium since the chances of any mishap/accident are higher.

  • Insurance Type

    A third-party insurance cover is cheap compared to a comprehensive bike insurance policy. This is because comprehensive insurance provides extensive financial coverage with additional benefits.

  • Anti-theft Safety Discount

    If an ARAI (Automotive Research Association of India) approved an anti-theft device fitted in your bike, then you can enjoy discounts on premium.
    At insurejoy.com, you can key in all the factors and reach the best premium value as per your needs, in just a few clicks!
